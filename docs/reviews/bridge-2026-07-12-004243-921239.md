## Codex (review)

1. **P1 must-fix — APD2/APD8 §4.3, AP2 — Verified-email auto-linking enables account takeover.** A recycled or reassigned email with a new provider `sub` is automatically attached to the prior user’s synced data. Google explicitly says email may change or be non-unique and must not identify users. **Fix:** identify by `(issuer, sub)` only; require an authenticated, reverified account-link flow; make email non-unique; replace the dedupe test with same-email isolation plus explicit-link tests. [Spec §4.3](/Users/mragile.io/dev/Claude/Projects/3dprintassistant/docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-11-accounts-platform-design.md:204), [Google guidance](https://developers.google.com/identity/openid-connect/openid-connect).

2. **P1 must-fix — §4.2–4.3, AP2/AP9 — Apple revocation loses the required client identifier.** Web grants use the Services ID; native grants use the bundle ID. Apple requires `/auth/revoke`’s `client_id` to match the authorization, but `oauth_identities` and `revocation_outbox` retain only the token. Revocation can therefore fail permanently, violating acceptance criterion 4. **Fix:** store each Apple token with its originating client ID, preserve that pair in the outbox, revoke every distinct client grant, and test web-only/native-only/both-client deletion. [Schema](/Users/mragile.io/dev/Claude/Projects/3dprintassistant/docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-11-accounts-platform-design.md:137), [native flow](/Users/mragile.io/dev/Claude/Projects/3dprintassistant/docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-11-accounts-platform-design.md:207), [Apple revocation contract](https://developer.apple.com/documentation/signinwithapplerestapi/revoke-tokens).
