Since the reviewer is stochastic and a `GO` is never re-examined, timed retries are repeated samples of a one-way gate: a machine for eventually getting past the block. Introduced by the controller in the B4 contract (`f38d270`), **not** by the reviewed v2 spec (which only retried environment-dependent reasons). The live contract's sentence literally contradicted itself, and the first run resolved the ambiguity toward the timer.
- **The invariant that finally killed it (after three failed patch attempts):** **NO-GO taint** — once a candidate has any `NO-GO` on record it may occupy only `judgment-on-evidence` or `decision-required`, and may reach a review turn ONLY via new external evidence satisfying RD3 or an explicit owner instruction. No timer, no repair lane, **no migration**. Guarded by a taxonomy-graph reachability test that **all three spec drafts would have failed**.
